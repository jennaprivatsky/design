GLO TAG TOY DESIGN
GLO TAG is a toy created during a Toy Product Design class at the University of Minnesota. The class was sponsored by Target, and the product was created to meet Target's guidelines.

GLO TAG works using RFID technology. Each player wears a pack and a hand strap. When a player touches their hand strap to the target on the pack of another player, the tagged player will lose a life. The lives are represented by the colored bars on the straps of the pack. Once all lives are lost, the pack will deactivate and the player will be out.

Construction of GLO TAG included 3 rounds of prototyping which required electronic programming, sewing, laser cutting, and design aesthetic.

GLO TAG was designed by a team including myself and 4 other students in the class.
